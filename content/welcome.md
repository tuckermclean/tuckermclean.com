I'm a Linux infrastructure and automation engineer based in the Portland, OR metro. I started in 1990 with a keyboard and BASIC and haven't stopped since.

**Currently at Intel (via Kelly OCG)**, I'm automating IT lab compute provisioning across Intel's Global IT Lab Compute using Ansible, AWX, and FOG, and building Grafana dashboards for provisioning metrics across lab environments.

**Previously at Intel (2021–2024)**, I built a lab network from scratch — 100G fiber, PXE, automated deployments — saving thousands by repurposing hardware nobody else thought to use. I owned firmware release pipelines and ran the full compliance stack (Coverity, Black Duck, Klocwork) as acting SWLC liaison for Cisco releases. I untangled Wireshark traces for global retailers and coordinated security advisories across product lines.

Before Intel, I shipped features for **Powercode** — ISP management software running 300+ providers across North America — as sole engineer on DHCP Option 82 and RADIUS integration.

On my own time, I build things that have no business existing. [**The Monolith**](https://github.com/tuckermclean/linux-live-iso-factory) cross-compiles a complete bootable Linux system targeting i486 through modern x86 — musl, static binaries, 30 years of hardware supported. [**Personliness**](https://github.com/tuckermclean/personliness) is a live full-stack personality platform scoring historical figures across 36 original psychometric traits, LLM-integrated, deployed on my self-hosted K3s cluster.

I'm fluent in Linux, Python, Terraform, Ansible, AWS, Docker, Kubernetes, and Node.js. I see systems whole — technical and organizational — and I know what to fix first.
